A Remarkable Day for SSCn's at Microsoft Office

Date: February 15, 2025
Location: Microsoft Office, Gachibowli

Yesterday was an unforgettable day as I had the incredible opportunity to attend a Microsoft Office event with the Student Service Community Network (SSCN). We had representatives from Chennai, Anantapur district (Andhra Pradesh), Hyderabad, and IIT Madras. The event saw over 90 registrations, with 25 confirmed attendees and 20 participants finally joining us at Microsoft.

The Journey: Laughter & Challenges

The journey itself was an adventure!

  • Starting Point: I, along with Sai Akash, began our trip from Machilipatnam. We were later joined by Gayathri, Sreelakshmi, Yashwanth, Dileep, and Madhan at Gudivada Station.
  • Train Delay: Our train to Secunderabad was delayed by two hours, so we reached at 7:45 AM and took the metro.
  • Ticket Mishap: I mistakenly bought metro tickets for only 7 people, leaving Sreelakshmi without a ticket. However, we managed the situation quickly and had a fun time navigating the metro.

One crazy moment was squeezing 8 people into a single auto to reach Microsoft Gachibowli. It was chaotic but incredibly fun!

Hurdles at Microsoft

Upon reaching Gate 4 at Microsoft Office, some challenges awaited us:

  • Sreelakshmi's Missing Confirmation: She didn’t have the confirmation ticket, but I managed to get her inside.
  • Nandyal Team's Delay: The mentors and SSCn's from Nandyal arrived late and weren’t allowed in. After much effort and with Swathi’s mother’s support, 5 members, including 4 SSC mentors, were able to attend.

    • I thank to Swathi's Mother For her unwavering support, I handed over one of the medals I received during the event to Swathi’s mother. It was a token of appreciation for her fight, her belief in us, and her role in ensuring the SSC mentors didn’t miss out on this life-changing experience. Words like "thank you" felt too small for her contribution, so this medal now symbolizes our respect and gratitude.

Key Highlights of the Event

Upon reaching Gate 4 at Microsoft Office, some challenges awaited us:

  • Networking: I connected with industry leaders like Morten Knudsen, Praveen Singampalli, and Kamal Shree. The SSC Community was widely recognized, and many participants appreciated our work.
  • Recognition: I answered multiple questions during the sessions, earning two medals and a t-shirt.
  • Memorable Lunch: We had a delicious lunch and clicked team photos to capture the day’s memories.

Key Sessions & Learnings

The event featured a series of insightful sessions led by industry experts and Microsoft MVPs. Here are some key takeaways:

Scaling the Unscalable

Siddharth Vijay (AVP, Engineering | Baazi Games)

Explored strategies for building and managing scalable, high-concurrency gaming platforms with a strong focus on reliability and security.

Get Sh*t Done in IT

Michael Seidl (Owner | Microsoft MVP)

Delivered practical insights to boost IT productivity and streamline operations in real-world environments.

CLI for Microsoft 365

Arjun Menon (Microsoft MVP)

Demonstrated the power of CLI tools to enhance productivity across Microsoft 365 workloads.

AI Integration with DevOps

Praveen Singampalli (Mentor | HeyDevOps)

Revealed how AI can automate DevOps workflows, improve decision-making, and increase efficiency.

Future of Observability

Zameer Fouzan (Lead Developer Relations | New Relic)

Provided insights into OpenTelemetry and the evolving landscape of observability within the Microsoft ecosystem.

Azure & Security Copilot Demos

Morten Waltorp Knudsen (MVP | 2LinkIT)

Offered hands-on demonstrations showcasing how Copilot tools enhance cloud performance and security.

PowerShell Meets GitHub Copilot

Stoyan Chalakov (Sr. Consultant | Microsoft MVP)

Shared expert tips on maximizing coding efficiency by integrating GitHub Copilot with PowerShell scripting.

Challenges Along the Way

The journey was not without its difficulties. A two-hour train delay from Machilipatnam to Secunderabad disrupted our planned itinerary. Additionally, a metro ticket issue left one of our team members, Sreelakshmi, without valid access, and entry complications at Microsoft’s security gate added further pressure. These moments tested our patience and problem-solving abilities.

Turning the Tide

Despite the obstacles, we remained resilient. Quick thinking resolved last-minute ticketing issues, and support from Swathi’s mother proved crucial in assisting the Nandyal team. Our commitment and active engagement during the sessions were recognized, earning us medals and applause — a true testament to our perseverance and teamwork.

Why Attend Events Like This?

Following the event, we gathered for a mentor debrief session to reflect on key takeaways:

  • Such events are transformative — they shift perspectives, spark innovation, and create valuable opportunities for internships, career growth, and professional networking.
  • They help foster strong communities and build lasting connections that can shape our future in tech.

The Journey Back: A Crazy Adventure

After bidding farewell to Microsoft, our return journey turned into an unexpected adventure. With no bus tickets available, we boarded a general train. Despite the exhaustion, the ride was filled with laughter, thoughtful conversations, and a few lighthearted ego clashes — the kind that bring people closer.

Ending on a High Note

The most heartwarming surprise came when the SSC mentors presented a thoughtful birthday gift — a moment that made me feel truly seen and appreciated. It was the perfect ending to a memorable day.

This experience has strengthened my ambition to work at Microsoft one day, and it deepened my commitment to growing the Student Service Community (SSC) with even greater passion and purpose.

Gratitude to the Microsoft team, SSC mentors, and every individual who contributed to making this day so impactful and unforgettable.
